Difficult Words:>

i) **Innate**

 **English Meaning**: Existing naturally or inherently; a quality or characteristic present from birth rather than acquired.
 **Hindi Meaning**: प्राकृतिक रूप से मौजूद या जन्मजात; एक गुण या विशेषता जो जन्म से होती है और सीखी नहीं जाती।

 **Synonyms**: inborn, inherent, natural, intrinsic, instinctive
 **Antonyms**: acquired, learned, external, extrinsic, unnatural

 **Examples**:
   "She has an innate talent for music that was evident from a young age."
   "His innate sense of kindness made him popular among his friends."

ii) **Deliberate**

 **English Meaning**: Done intentionally or with careful consideration; to think about or discuss something carefully.
 **Hindi Meaning**: जानबूझकर या सावधानीपूर्वक विचार के साथ किया गया; किसी चीज़ के बारे में सोचसमझकर या चर्चा करना।

 **Synonyms**: intentional, purposeful, calculated, planned, premeditated
 **Antonyms**: accidental, unintentional, impulsive, hasty, spontaneous

 **Examples**:
   "The team made a deliberate decision after discussing all possible options."
   "Her actions were deliberate, showing that she had thought them through carefully."

iii) **Adamant**

 **English Meaning**: Refusing to change one’s mind or be persuaded; firmly determined.
 **Hindi Meaning**: अपने विचार को बदलने या समझाने के लिए तैयार नहीं होना; दृढ़ निश्चयी।

 **Synonyms**: unyielding, stubborn, resolute, determined, unwavering
 **Antonyms**: flexible, yielding, willing, open, agreeable

 **Examples**:
   "Despite the criticism, he was adamant about his decision to pursue art."
   "She remained adamant that she would not attend the event."

iv) **Meticulous**

 **English Meaning**: Showing great attention to detail; being very careful and precise in doing something.
 **Hindi Meaning**: किसी चीज़ को करते समय बहुत ध्यान देना; अत्यंत सावधानीपूर्वक और सटीक होना।

 **Synonyms**: careful, precise, thorough, detailed, exacting
 **Antonyms**: careless, sloppy, hasty, imprecise, negligent

 **Examples**:
   "She is meticulous in her work, ensuring that every detail is perfect."
   "The architect's meticulous planning led to a flawless building design."

v) **Grueling**

 **English Meaning**: Extremely tiring and demanding; requiring a lot of effort, often exhausting.
 **Hindi Meaning**: अत्यधिक थकाने वाला और कठिन; जिसमें बहुत प्रयास की आवश्यकता होती है, अक्सर थका देने वाला होता है।

 **Synonyms**: exhausting, demanding, strenuous, tough, arduous
 **Antonyms**: easy, effortless, simple, relaxing, gentle

 **Examples**:
   "The marathon was a grueling test of endurance and strength."
   "She completed the grueling workday, exhausted but satisfied."

vi) **Obsession**

 **English Meaning**: A persistent and intense focus or preoccupation with a particular thought, object, or activity that is hard to let go of.
 **Hindi Meaning**: किसी विशेष विचार, वस्तु, या गतिविधि के प्रति लगातार और अत्यधिक ध्यान देना, जिसे छोड़ना मुश्किल हो।

 **Synonyms**: fixation, compulsion, fascination, preoccupation, infatuation
 **Antonyms**: indifference, apathy, detachment, disinterest, disregard

 **Examples**:
   "His obsession with perfection often caused him to redo his work multiple times."
   "She developed an obsession with fitness and started working out every day."

v) **Splurge**

 **English Meaning**: To spend money freely or extravagantly, often on something indulgent or unnecessary.
 **Hindi Meaning**: पैसे को स्वतंत्र रूप से या बहुत खर्च करना, अक्सर किसी ऐसी चीज़ पर जो विलासिता या गैरज़रूरी हो।

 **Synonyms**: indulge, lavish, spend, treat, splurge out
 **Antonyms**: save, conserve, economize, hoard, budget

 **Examples**:
   "She decided to splurge on a luxury handbag after her promotion."
   "They splurged on a fancy dinner to celebrate their anniversary."

vi) **Pursuit**

 **English Meaning**: The act of chasing or seeking something; an endeavor or activity that one engages in to achieve a goal or interest.
 **Hindi Meaning**: किसी चीज़ का पीछा करने या खोजने की क्रिया; एक प्रयास या गतिविधि जिसमें कोई लक्ष्य या रुचि प्राप्त करने के लिए संलग्न होता है।

 **Synonyms**: quest, chase, pursuit, endeavor, hunt
 **Antonyms**: avoidance, neglect, retreat, abandonment, idleness

 **Examples**:
   "His pursuit of happiness led him to travel around the world."
   "They are in pursuit of a solution to the environmental crisis."

vii) **Quest**

 **English Meaning**: A long or arduous search for something; a pursuit or journey undertaken to achieve a goal or find something of value.
 **Hindi Meaning**: किसी चीज़ के लिए लंबी या कठिन खोज; एक लक्ष्य प्राप्त करने या किसी मूल्यवान चीज़ को खोजने के लिए की गई यात्रा या प्रयास।

 **Synonyms**: search, journey, expedition, pursuit, endeavor
 **Antonyms**: abandonment, idleness, retreat, neglect, giving up

 **Examples**:
   "The knight embarked on a quest to find the lost treasure."
   "Her quest for knowledge led her to read countless books."

viii) **Unbeknownst**

 **English Meaning**: Without the knowledge of; unknown or not known to someone.
 **Hindi Meaning**: किसी के ज्ञान के बिना; किसी के लिए अज्ञात या न ज्ञात।

 **Synonyms**: unknown, unnoticed, unsuspected, unrecognized, unobserved
 **Antonyms**: known, recognized, acknowledged, aware, informed

 **Examples**:
   "Unbeknownst to her, a surprise party was being planned for her birthday."
   "He made decisions that would impact the team, unbeknownst to the other members."

ix) **Bewildered**

 **English Meaning**: Confused or perplexed; having trouble understanding something or being unable to think clearly.
 **Hindi Meaning**: भ्रमित या चकित; किसी चीज़ को समझने में कठिनाई होना या स्पष्ट रूप से सोचने में असमर्थ होना।

 **Synonyms**: confused, perplexed, baffled, puzzled, astonished
 **Antonyms**: clear, composed, certain, informed, aware

 **Examples**:
   "She felt bewildered by the sudden change in plans."
   "His bewildered expression showed that he didn't understand the question."

x)**Ruse**

 **English Meaning**: A cunning plan or trick designed to deceive someone; a deceptive maneuver or strategy.
 **Hindi Meaning**: किसी को धोखा देने के लिए बनाई गई एक चालाक योजना या तरकीब; एक धोखाधड़ी का तरीका या रणनीति।

 **Synonyms**: trick, ploy, deception, stratagem, subterfuge
 **Antonyms**: honesty, truth, straightforwardness, sincerity, openness

 **Examples**:
   "The detective saw through the suspect's ruse and caught him in the act."
   "She used a clever ruse to distract her opponents during the game."

xi) **Agitation**

 **English Meaning**: A state of anxiety or nervousness; the act of stirring up public interest or concern; intense emotional disturbance or unrest.
 **Hindi Meaning**: चिंता या नर्वसनेस की स्थिति; जनहित या चिंता को उकसाने की क्रिया; तीव्र भावनात्मक अशांति या अशांति।

 **Synonyms**: unrest, disturbance, turmoil, anxiety, excitement
 **Antonyms**: calm, peace, serenity, tranquility, stability

 **Examples**:
   "Her agitation was evident as she waited for the test results."
   "The community organized a protest in agitation against the new policy."


What I have learned today :--> 

* The culture we live in determines which behaviour are attractive to us.
* We tend to adopt habits that are praised and approved of by our culture because we have a strong desire to fit in and belong to the tribe.
* We tend to imitate the habits of three social groups: the close(family and friends), the many(the tribe), and the powerful(those with status and prestige).
* One of the most effective things you can do to build better habits is to join a culture where (1) your desired behaviour is the normal behaviour and (2) you already have something in common with the group.
* The normal behaviour of the tribe often overpowers the desired behaviour of the individual. Most days, we'd rather be wrong with the crowd than be right ourselves.
* If a behaviour can get us approval, respect, and praise, we find it attractive.
